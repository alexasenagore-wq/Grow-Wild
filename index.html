What's Going On in the Garden

The latest happenings from around the yard. Check back seasonally for updates!

Spring

An early and unique bloomer: Prairie Smoke

Prairie smoke is one of the earliest species to bloom in the spring. After it flowers, the flower heads look like troll hair! This is one of the most surreal looking plants in my garden and is essential in feeding early waking pollinators each spring.

Late Spring

Late Spring Brings Gorgeous Peony Blooms

Peonies are a lovely early spring bloomer. I have a variety of peonies throughout the yard.

Summer

Planting Coneflower now for blooms later

I'm planting coneflower seeds now because they require no germination period and will awaken in the late summer.

Summer

Butterfly Weed in Bloom. Spot the Bee Butt!

Butterfly weed (Asclepias tuberosa) is a milkweed that pollinators go absolutely wild for.

Late Spring

Foxglove Beardtongue

I love seeing hummingbirds visit these blooms and the bee butts climbing inside to collect pollen!

Spotted!

Monarch Caterpillar on Milkweed

This monarch caterpillar means the milkweed is doing exactly what it's supposed to do. Providing a host plant for the next generation of monarchs.

Hate Mosquitos? Try a bucket!

I'm doing my part to reduce mosquitos by using a mosquito bucket. It's the only way to actually kill mosquitos (fogging does not work) and does not harm other beneficial insects or animals. Let's have a mosquito free summer by working together to use mosquito buckets around the neighborhood!

Join the Mosquito Bucket Challenge!

Featured Visitors

Monarch Waystation

You'll notice monarchs both visiting my plants as well as laying eggs on their milkweed host plants.

New garden resident! The Black Swallowtail

I've been trying to attract swallowtails for 3 years and it finally happened! You'll see them around the Golden Alexanders, one of their host plants.

Firefly

I'm trying to increase the amount of fireflies in the yard by avoiding fogging services and leaving leaf litter for them in the garden beds.

Haploa reversa
